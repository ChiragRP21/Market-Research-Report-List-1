<p><p>The Acoustic Metamaterial market encompasses three main types: resonant membrane, periodic resonators, and sonic crystal. Resonant membrane metamaterials are thin materials that vibrate at specific frequencies to manipulate sound waves. Periodic resonators are structures with repeated units that control sound by reflecting or transmitting specific frequencies. Sonic crystals are materials with a unique arrangement of resonators that can block or manipulate sound waves at different frequencies. These types of acoustic metamaterials offer innovative solutions for controlling and directing sound in various applications such as noise reduction, soundproofing, and ultrasound imaging.</p></p>
