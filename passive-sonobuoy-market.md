<p><p>Passive sonobuoys are acoustic listening devices used in underwater surveillance to detect and track submarines or other marine vessels. There are two types of passive sonobuoys: wired sonobuoys and wireless sonobuoys. Wired sonobuoys are connected to the deploying aircraft or ship via a cable, allowing for real-time communication and data transmission. Wireless sonobuoys, on the other hand, utilize radio signals to send the collected acoustic data to the deploying platform without the need for physical cables. Both types play a crucial role in anti-submarine warfare by enhancing situational awareness and aiding in target localization.</p></p>
