<p><p>Bipolar transistors are electronic devices used for amplification and switching purposes. They are classified into two main types: PNP and NPN. PNP transistors consist of a layer of P-doped semiconductor sandwiched between two N-doped layers, while NPN transistors have a layer of N-doped semiconductor sandwiched between two P-doped layers. These transistors differ in their current flow direction and voltage polarities. PNP transistors allow current to flow from emitter to base, while NPN transistors allow current to flow from base to emitter. Both types are widely used in various electronic devices and circuits.</p></p>
