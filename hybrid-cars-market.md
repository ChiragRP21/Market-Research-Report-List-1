<p><p>Hybrid cars have different market types based on their powertrain configuration. In a series hybrid, the engine generates electricity that powers an electric motor, which propels the vehicle. In a parallel hybrid, both the engine and electric motor work together to propel the vehicle. A plug-in hybrid can be charged from an external power source and uses both electricity and gasoline to run. A series-parallel hybrid combines the features of series and parallel hybrids, allowing the car to be driven by either the engine or electric motor or a combination of both, depending on the driving conditions.</p></p>
