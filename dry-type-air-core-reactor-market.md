<p><p>A dry type air-core reactor is a type of reactor that contains windings carrying alternating current (AC) that are not immersed in any insulating liquid or gas. The windings are made of conductive material such as aluminum or copper, and are encased in a non-magnetic container made of materials like epoxy resin or another suitable insulating material. These reactors are commonly used in power transmission and distribution systems to control current flows, limit voltage fluctuations, and to improve power quality.</p><p>The dry type air-core reactor market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.7% during the forecast period.
