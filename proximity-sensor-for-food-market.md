<p><p>Proximity sensors, commonly used in the food market, are devices that detect the presence or absence of objects within a certain range without making any physical contact. Inductive proximity sensors utilize an electromagnetic field to detect metallic objects. Capacitive proximity sensors work by measuring changes in capacitance between their electrodes and the target object. Magnetic proximity sensors utilize a magnetic field to detect magnetic materials. These sensors play a crucial role in automating processes within the food industry, ensuring precise and efficient detection of objects and enhancing overall productivity.</p></p>
