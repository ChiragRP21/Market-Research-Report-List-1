<p><p>The engine fastener market comprises three main types: bolts, screws, and nuts. Bolts provide a threaded fastening system, typically used to secure two or more components together. Screws are similar to bolts but differ in their design, often used to secure objects in a rotating motion. Nuts function as a counterpart to bolts, providing a threaded hole for fastening purposes. These fasteners play a crucial role in securing engine components and ensuring the overall performance and durability of the engine system.</p></p>
