<p><p>Metal forming in the automotive market involves various techniques to shape metal components. Roll forming is a continuous process where metal is gradually bent into a desired shape. Stretch forming involves stretching a metal sheet over a die to form complex curves. Stamping uses a die to press metal sheets into specific shapes. Deep drawing pulls metal sheets into a die cavity to create deep, hollow parts. Hydroforming uses fluid pressure to shape metal tubes into various forms. Other techniques include spinning, flow forming, and incremental sheet forming.</p></p>
