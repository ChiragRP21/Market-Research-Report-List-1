<p><p>High-duty refractory refers to materials with high resistance to heat, corrosion, and mechanical stress. The market for such refractories can be categorized into two types: clay-based and non clay-based. Clay-based refractories are made primarily from clay materials and possess excellent thermal stability, making them suitable for applications in industries such as steel, cement, and glass. Non clay-based refractories, on the other hand, are made from materials like silica, alumina, magnesia, and carbon and are known for their superior resistance to extreme temperatures, acids, and alkalis, making them ideal for applications in industries such as petrochemicals and ceramics.</p></p>
