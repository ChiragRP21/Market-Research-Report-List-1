Three-way oxidizing catalytic converters are designed to reduce three types of pollutants, namely carbon monoxide (CO), nitrogen oxides (NOx), and unburned hydrocarbons (HC). On the other hand, two-way oxidizing catalytic converters are designed to reduce only carbon monoxide and unburned hydrocarbons. Both types of converters play a crucial role in minimizing air pollution and ensuring compliance with emissions regulations.</p></p>
