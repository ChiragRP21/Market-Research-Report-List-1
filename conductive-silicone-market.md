<p><p>Conductive silicone is used in various applications due to its unique electrical conductivity properties. It is available in different forms, namely elastomers, resins, and gels. Elastomers are flexible and stretchable materials that are commonly used in products requiring flexibility and durability. Resins, on the other hand, are rigid and solid materials suitable for applications requiring high dimensional stability. Lastly, gels are semi-solid materials known for their soft and gel-like consistency, making them ideal for applications that require adherence or encapsulation. Overall, these different types of conductive silicone cater to a wide range of requirements in the market.</p></p>
