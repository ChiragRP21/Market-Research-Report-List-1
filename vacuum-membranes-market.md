<p><p>Vacuum membranes are specialized membranes that are used in various industrial applications such as water and wastewater treatment, food and beverage processing, pharmaceutical and biotechnology, chemicals, and oil and gas refining. Factors such as the increasing need for clean water, stringent government regulations regarding water and wastewater treatment, and the rising demand for efficient separation technologies are driving the market growth. Nanofiltration membranes offer greater selectivity compared to other membrane types, allowing them to selectively remove certain ions and molecules. This makes them suitable for various applications such as desalination, pharmaceutical manufacturing, and separation of organic matter. The increasing focus on sustainability and the need for higher quality products are driving the usage of nanofiltration membranes.</p><p>Another emerging trend is the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and automation in vacuum membrane systems.
