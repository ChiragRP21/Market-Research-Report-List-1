<p><ul><li>Rotational</li><li>Torsional Oscillation</li><li>Vibration</li><li>Moving Piston</li><li>Coriolis</li><li>Dynamic Fluid Pressure</li><li>Acoustic Wave (Solid-State)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The In-Line Process Viscometer (ILPV) market comprises various types of viscometers used for measuring the viscosity of liquids. Rotational viscometers involve rotating a spindle immersed in the fluid. Torsional oscillation viscometers measure the resistance to twisting of a vibrating rod in the liquid. Vibration viscometers analyze the damping effect of liquid on a vibrating element. Moving piston viscometers use the pressure drop across a piston moving through the fluid. Coriolis viscometers measure the change in fluid momentum. Dynamic fluid pressure viscometers determine viscosity based on fluid pressure drop. Acoustic wave viscometers analyze the speed of sound waves in the liquid via solid-state technology.</p></p>
