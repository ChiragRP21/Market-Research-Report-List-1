<p><p>Car ferries are categorized into two main types: monohull and multihull. Monohull ferries have a single hull or body structure, usually resembling a traditional ship with a smooth and rounded shape. On the other hand, multihull ferries have two or more hulls, generally catamaran or trimaran designs, which provide greater stability and enable higher speeds. While monohull ferries are more common and suitable for shorter distances, multihull ferries are preferred for longer journeys and rougher waters due to their improved stability and efficiency.</p></p>
