<p><p>Analytical HPLC systems consist of various market types, including normal phase HPLC and reverse phase HPLC. Normal phase HPLC is a technique that separates analytes based on their polarity differences, with the stationary phase being polar and the mobile phase being non-polar. On the other hand, reverse phase HPLC separates analytes based on their hydrophobicity, with the stationary phase being non-polar and the mobile phase being polar. These market types offer different approaches to chromatographic separation, allowing scientists to effectively analyze a wide range of compounds in different sample matrices.</p></p>
