<p><p>Cassava starch is classified into two main market types: native starch and modified starch. Native starch refers to the starch extracted directly from cassava root without any chemical or physical modifications. It is commonly used in food and beverage industries due to its excellent thickening and binding properties. On the other hand, modified starch is chemically or physically altered to enhance its performance under specific conditions, such as in frozen food products or as a stabilizer in dairy products. Modified starch offers improved functionality and thus finds wide applications in various industries.</p></p>
