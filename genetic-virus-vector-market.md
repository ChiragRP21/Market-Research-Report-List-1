<p><p>Genetic virus vectors are used in gene therapy to deliver therapeutic genes to target cells. Retroviral vectors are derived from retroviruses, enabling stable gene expression. Adenoviral vectors are derived from adenoviruses, allowing efficient gene delivery to a wide range of dividing and non-dividing cells. Adeno-associated viral vectors are derived from adeno-associated viruses, providing long-term gene expression with minimal immune response. Other vectors, including lentiviral and herpes simplex viral vectors, also offer unique advantages for gene therapy. These diverse market types cater to different therapeutic needs and target cell types, expanding the possibilities for gene therapy treatments.</p></p>
