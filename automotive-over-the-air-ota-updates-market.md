<p><p>Automotive Over the Air (OTA) Updates market includes two types of updates known as Software Over the Air (SOTA) and Firmware Over the Air (FOTA) updates. SOTA refers to the wireless transmission of software updates to a vehicle's electronic control units (ECUs) to enhance functionality or fix bugs. FOTA, on the other hand, involves updating a vehicle's embedded software, including system firmware and drivers. Both SOTA and FOTA updates allow automotive manufacturers to remotely update the software and firmware of vehicles, improving performance, safety, and user experience without requiring physical intervention or visits to service centers.</p></p>
