<p><p>Micro and Mechanized Irrigation Systems are advanced irrigation techniques used to deliver water efficiently to crops. One of the emerging trends is the integration of digital technologies, such as IoT and AI, in irrigation systems. These technologies enable remote monitoring and control of irrigation systems, resulting in optimized water usage and improved crop yield.</p><p>Another trend is the adoption of precision irrigation techniques, which involve customized water application based on crop type, soil moisture levels, and weather conditions. <p><p>The micro and mechanized irrigation systems market includes various types of irrigation techniques. Sprinkler irrigation involves distributing water through sprinklers, ensuring an even and uniform coverage over the crops. Drip irrigation utilizes a network of pipes with small emitters that deliver water directly to the roots of plants, reducing water loss and improving efficiency. Central pivot irrigation systems have a moving central pivot supplying water to the crops in a circular pattern. Lateral move irrigation systems move along a straight line, delivering water to the fields as they travel.</p></p>
