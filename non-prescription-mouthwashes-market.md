<p><p>The non-prescription mouthwash market can be categorized into two main types: alcoholic and non-alcoholic mouthwashes. Alcoholic mouthwashes usually contain a high percentage of ethanol, which acts as an antiseptic, helping to kill bacteria and germs in the mouth. These mouthwashes may have a strong burning sensation and are typically recommended for those with gum diseases or bad breath. On the other hand, non-alcoholic mouthwashes are alcohol-free and often contain alternative ingredients like fluoride or oxygen-based compounds. They are milder and suitable for individuals with sensitive teeth, dry mouth, or those seeking fresher breath without the burning sensation.</p></p>
