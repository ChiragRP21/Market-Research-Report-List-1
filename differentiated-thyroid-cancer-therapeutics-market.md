<p><p>Differentiated thyroid cancer, or DTC, is an aggressive form of thyroid cancer. The therapeutics market for DTC includes various treatment types. Radioiodine ablation involves using radioactive iodine to destroy remaining cancer cells after surgery. Thyroid-stimulating hormone suppression aims to reduce thyroid hormone levels to inhibit cancer growth. Chemotherapy uses drugs to kill cancer cells throughout the body. Targeted multikinase therapy targets specific molecules involved in cancer growth. Other therapies may include immunotherapy, external radiation therapy, or palliative care to relieve symptoms. These treatments can be used individually or in combination to effectively manage DTC.</p></p>
