<p><ul><li>Air Cooling</li><li>Liquid Cooling</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The New Energy Vehicle Motor Thermal Management market includes two types of cooling systems: air cooling and liquid cooling. </p><p>Air cooling involves the use of air to dissipate heat generated by the vehicle motor. It uses fans or blowers to circulate the air and remove heat from the motor components. This method is simpler and cheaper but may have limitations in extreme temperature conditions.</p><p>Liquid cooling, on the other hand, utilizes a liquid coolant to absorb and transfer heat away from the motor. It involves a system of pipes, pumps, and heat exchangers to carry the coolant and dissipate the heat. This method provides more efficient cooling and is suitable for demanding and high-performance applications.</p></p>
