<p><p>Microsurgery Electrosurgical Units (ESUs) are devices used in surgical procedures that utilize electrical currents to cut, coagulate, and seal tissues. There are three main types of ESUs available in the market. Monopole Electrosurgical Units use a single active electrode to deliver electrical energy for tissue manipulation. Bipolar Electrosurgical Units have both the active and return electrodes present on the surgical instrument, allowing for precise and controlled tissue coagulation. Vessel Sealing Units are specialized ESUs that are designed specifically for sealing blood vessels securely during surgery to minimize bleeding and improve patient outcomes.</p></p>
