<p><p>Carboxymethyl cellulose sodium (CMC-Na) is a versatile and widely used derivative of cellulose, which is derived from plant-based materials such as wood pulp or cotton fibers. The market is expected to witness steady growth, primarily driven by the increasing demand for CMC-Na in the food and beverages industry. CMC-Na is extensively used as a stabilizer and texture enhancer in processed foods such as dairy products, sauces, and baked goods. With the growing popularity of convenience foods and the need for improved texture and stability, the demand for CMC-Na is expected to rise.</p><p>Moreover, the pharmaceutical industry is also likely to contribute to the growth of the CMC-Na market. The use of CMC-Na as a binder, disintegrant, and suspending agent in pharmaceutical formulations is increasing, especially in tablet manufacturing. As the demand for pharmaceuticals continues to rise globally, it is expected to positively impact the market for CMC-Na.</p><p>Additionally, the increasing consumer awareness regarding personal care and hygiene products is expected to drive the demand for CMC-Na in the personal care industry. It is used in various products such as creams, lotions, and shampoos due to its water-binding and thickening properties.</p><p>Overall, with the growing demand for processed foods, pharmaceuticals, and personal care products worldwide, the carboxymethyl cellulose sodium market is poised to grow at a CAGR of 6.7% during the forecasted period.</p></p>

<p><p>The Carboxymethyl Cellulose Sodium (CMC) market is categorized into three types based on viscosity levels. </p><p>1. Low Viscosity: This type of CMC, when dissolved in a 2% solution, exhibits viscosity ranging from 0.2 to 0.5Pa·s. It is suitable for applications requiring low thickness or flow resistance.</p><p>2. Medium Viscosity: CMC with medium viscosity ranges from 0.3 to 0.6Pa·s when dissolved in a 2% solution. It is commonly used in industries requiring moderate thickness or flow properties.</p><p>3. High Viscosity: With a range of 0.8 to 1.0Pa·s in a 2% solution, high viscosity CMC is used in applications that demand thickening or high flow resistance.</p></p>
