ASM involves the use of coatings that release antibacterial agents, actively preventing bacterial colonization. PSM refers to the modification of the implant's surface to make it less susceptible to bacterial attachment. LCC involves the application of localized coatings or carriers that gradually release antibacterial agents around the implant site. These treatments aim to reduce the risk of infection and improve the overall performance of orthopedic implants.</p></p>
