<p><p>The bio-composites market encompasses various types of manufacturing techniques. Extrusion involves pushing a raw bio-composite material through a die to create a continuous product with a constant cross-sectional shape. Compression molding involves placing the bio-composite material into a mold cavity and applying pressure until it takes the desired shape. Injection molding employs high pressure to inject the bio-composite material into a mold cavity, resulting in a precise and complex product. Other manufacturing techniques may include thermoforming, pultrusion, and rotational molding, each offering different advantages based on the desired end product.</p></p>
