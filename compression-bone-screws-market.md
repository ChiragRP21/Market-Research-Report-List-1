<p><p>Compression bone screws are commonly used in orthopedic surgeries to fix bones in place. They come in various types, including cortical screws, cancellous screws, and others. Cortical screws are designed for denser bones and have a threaded shaft for secure fixation. Cancellous screws have a larger thread pitch and are used for spongy or weaker bones. Other types of compression bone screws include cannulated screws, headless screws, and self-tapping screws. Each type serves a specific purpose and is chosen based on the nature of the bone and the surgical requirements.</p></p>
