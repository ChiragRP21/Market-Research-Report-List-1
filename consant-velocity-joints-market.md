<p><p>Constant Velocity Joints (CV joints) are essential components of a vehicle's drivetrain, providing smooth power transmission to the wheels. The market for CV joints can be divided into two types: inner joints and outer joints. Inner joints are located close to the transmission, while outer joints are situated at the wheels. Inner joints transmit rotational force from the engine to the wheels, allowing the vehicle to move forward or backward. Outer joints are responsible for transferring power from the differential to the wheels, enabling smooth steering movements. Both inner and outer joints are crucial for maintaining a constant velocity and ensuring efficient vehicle operation.</p></p>
