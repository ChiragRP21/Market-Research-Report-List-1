<p><p>The gastrointestinal electrocardiograph market consists of two types: Electrogastroenterography (EGEG) and Cutaneous Electrogastrography. EGEG involves measuring the electrical signals generated by the muscles of the digestive system using invasive electrodes placed on the abdominal wall. This method helps monitor and diagnose gastrointestinal disorders. On the other hand, Cutaneous Electrogastrography involves non-invasive electrodes placed on the skin of the abdomen to measure gastric electrical activity. This method is also used for the assessment of gastrointestinal motility disorders. Both techniques aid in evaluating the functioning of the digestive system.</p></p>
