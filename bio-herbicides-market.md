<p><ul><li>Microbials</li><li>Biochemicals</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Bio-Herbicides market is divided into three types: Microbials, Biochemicals, and Others. Microbials refer to naturally occurring microorganisms, such as bacteria or fungi, that are used to control weeds. They work by infecting the target weed and inhibiting its growth. Biochemicals are organic compounds extracted from plants, which are formulated into herbicides. They act by disrupting the weed's metabolic processes. The "Others" category includes any other bio-herbicide types, such as enzymes or plant extracts, that work through different mechanisms to control weed growth.</p></p>
