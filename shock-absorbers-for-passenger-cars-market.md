<p><p>Shock absorbers are an essential part of a passenger car's suspension system that help dampen the impact and vibrations caused by uneven roads. Single-tube shock absorbers have a simple design with a single pressure tube and a piston to control the movement of the suspension. On the other hand, twin-tube shock absorbers have an additional outer tube called the reservoir tube that helps dissipate heat and improve performance. Both types serve the purpose of providing a smooth and comfortable ride by absorbing and controlling the energy from the road.</p></p>
